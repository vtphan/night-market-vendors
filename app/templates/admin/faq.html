Registration Playbook — When to do what and why
A new registration comes in

Open it from the dashboard. Review the vendor's information and booth selection. Approve or Reject — the vendor is emailed automatically.

Approving immediately reserves a booth slot, even before payment. Only approve if you're confident the vendor should get that spot.

An approved vendor hasn't paid yet

This is the biggest operational risk. The system sends payment reminders automatically based on your deadline settings, but there is no auto-expiration. If a vendor is holding a slot too long, you can revoke their approval (reverts to Pending, freeing the slot) or reject them outright.

A vendor pays

The registration moves to Paid automatically and the vendor receives a confirmation email. Spot-check the Stripe Dashboard periodically to confirm payments are processing correctly — especially if a vendor reports an issue or the status doesn't update after a few minutes.

A booth type is full

You cannot approve more vendors for that type. The dashboard shows current availability. To free a slot, reject or cancel an existing Approved or Paid registration.

A vendor needs to change their registration

Vendors cannot edit after submitting. You can change their category from the registration detail page. For other changes, the vendor must withdraw and resubmit (or you reject and they resubmit).

You need to cancel a paid registration

Open the registration and click Cancel. Select a refund percentage and reason. The refund is processed through Stripe automatically. The refund applies only to the booth fee — the processing fee is not refunded. Stripe's own fee on the original charge is also non-recoverable.

To cancel without a refund, select 0%. To customize refund options, edit "Refund Presets" in Admin Settings.

You made a mistake — can you undo?

Approval: Revert to Pending (frees the booth slot) or move to Rejected.
Rejection: Revert to Pending for re-review.
Payment: Cannot be undone — Cancel the registration instead (triggers a refund).

A vendor withdrew on their own

No action needed. Withdrawal is permanent and the booth slot (if any) is freed immediately. You'll receive an email notification if admin notifications are enabled.

A vendor filed a chargeback

You'll receive an urgent alert email. Respond to the dispute in the Stripe Dashboard before the deadline (typically 7–21 days) or you automatically lose the funds.

A vendor says they didn't receive an email

Verify their email address on the registration and ask them to check spam. They can always log in to check their status directly. If the problem persists, contact the developer.

Insurance — How to review and manage vendor insurance
What do I need to check?

Each vendor must upload a liability insurance certificate. Check the insurance status on the registration detail page — it shows whether a document has been uploaded and whether it's been approved.

Insurance is per-email, not per-registration

Insurance is tied to the vendor's email address. One upload covers all of that vendor's registrations. Revoking insurance also affects all their registrations.

Insurance is independent from registration status

A vendor can be Paid with no insurance on file. The system does not block payment or approval based on insurance. It is your responsibility to follow up on compliance separately.

Exporting data

Use the Export link on the dashboard. The CSV includes all registrations with contact info, booth details, payment info, and admin notes. Review before sharing — admin notes and reversal reasons are included.

Refunds via Stripe Dashboard

We recommend issuing refunds through the app. If you refund directly in Stripe, a full refund cancels the registration automatically; a partial refund updates the amount but does not change the status.
