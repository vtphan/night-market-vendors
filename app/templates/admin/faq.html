Getting Started — Login, statuses, and basic workflow
How do I log in as an admin?

Enter your email on the login page and use the one-time passcode sent to you. No passwords. Your email must be registered as an admin by the developer.

What do the registration statuses mean?

How does the approval workflow work?

Register → Admin Approval → Payment. Vendors cannot pay until you approve them, giving you full control over who gets a booth.

How do I approve or reject a vendor?

Open the registration from the dashboard and use the Approve or Reject button. The vendor is emailed automatically.

How do I know how many booth spots are left?

The dashboard shows availability for each booth type. Available = total quantity minus Approved and Paid registrations.

Can a vendor submit multiple registrations?

Yes. Each is tracked independently — approve, reject, or cancel each one separately.

Booths & Status Changes — Inventory, undoing actions, and vendor withdrawal
Do Pending registrations take up booth inventory?

No. Only Approved and Paid registrations count. Review Pending registrations promptly to avoid over-committing.

What happens when I approve a vendor?

A booth slot is immediately reserved — even before the vendor pays. If it's the last slot, no further approvals for that booth type are possible until one is freed.

Can an approved vendor hold a spot without paying?

Yes — this is the biggest operational risk. There is no auto-expiration on Approved status. Monitor approved-but-unpaid registrations and follow up or revoke approval as needed.

What if a booth type is full?

You cannot approve more vendors for that type. To free a slot, reject or cancel an existing Approved or Paid registration.

How do I change booth types, prices, or quantities?

Go to Admin Settings. Price changes only affect future approvals — already approved/paid registrations keep their original price.

Can I change a vendor's category after they register?

Yes. Open the registration detail page and use the category dropdown.

Can I undo an approval, rejection, or payment?

Approval: Revert to Pending (frees the booth slot) or move to Rejected.
Rejection: Revert to Pending for re-review.
Payment: Cannot be undone directly — Cancel the registration instead, which triggers a Stripe refund.

Can a vendor withdraw their own registration?

Yes. Vendors can withdraw Pending or Approved registrations. Withdrawal is permanent and frees the booth slot immediately. Both the vendor and all admins are notified. Vendors cannot withdraw after payment — only an admin can cancel.

Payments, Fees & Refunds — How payments and refunds work
Do I need to collect payments manually?

No. Approved vendors receive an email with a payment link. Payments are handled automatically via Stripe.

What happens when a vendor pays?

The registration moves from Approved to Paid automatically and the vendor receives a confirmation email.

A vendor says they paid but the status still shows Approved.

There may be a short delay. Wait a few minutes and refresh. If unchanged, check the Stripe Dashboard to confirm the payment succeeded.

How do processing fees work?

You can pass Stripe's processing fee to vendors so the organization nets the full booth price. Configure in Admin Settings under "Payment & Fees." To absorb fees instead, set both the percentage and flat amount to 0. Fee changes affect any vendor who hasn't paid yet.

How do I issue a refund?

Open the Paid registration and click Cancel. Select a refund percentage and reason — the refund is processed through Stripe automatically. The refund applies only to the booth fee; the processing fee is not refunded. Stripe's own fee on the original charge is also non-recoverable.

To cancel without a refund, select 0%. To customize refund options, edit "Refund Presets" in Admin Settings (default: 100,75,50,25,0).

Can I issue a refund directly in the Stripe Dashboard?

We recommend using the app instead. If you refund in Stripe directly, a full refund cancels the registration automatically; a partial refund updates the amount but does not change the status.

What happens if a vendor files a chargeback?

You'll receive an urgent alert email. Respond to the dispute in the Stripe Dashboard before the deadline (typically 7–21 days) or you automatically lose the funds.

Vendor-facing emails are sent automatically and cannot be suppressed. If a vendor says they didn't get an email, verify their address and ask them to check spam.

Can I receive notifications when vendors take actions?

Yes. In Admin Settings, enable notifications for new registrations, payments, or insurance uploads. These are off by default and are sent to all admin emails.

Insurance — Uploads, approval, and how it relates to registrations
How does insurance work?

Vendors upload their liability insurance certificate through their registration page. Admins review and approve or revoke it.

Is insurance tied to a specific registration?

No. Insurance is tied to the vendor's email address. One upload covers all of that vendor's registrations.

Is insurance approval separate from registration approval?

Yes. A vendor can be Paid with no insurance on file — follow up on insurance compliance separately.

Event Settings & Export — Configuration, dates, and data export
How do I update event dates, registration window, or page content?

Go to Admin Settings. You can edit the event name, dates, registration window, banner text, contact email, page content, vendor agreement, and more. Changes take effect immediately.

What happens when registration closes?

New submissions are blocked, but vendors can still log in to check existing registrations and approved vendors can still pay.

Where does the banner text appear?

At the top of the homepage and vendor dashboard. Use it for time-sensitive announcements.

How do I export registration data?

Use the Export link on the dashboard. The CSV includes all registrations with contact info, booth details, payment info, and admin notes. Review before sharing externally.
