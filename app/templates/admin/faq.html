Getting Started — Login, statuses, and basic workflow
How do I log in as an admin?

Go to the login page and enter your email. You'll receive a one-time passcode — enter it to log in. There are no passwords. Your email must be registered as an admin by the site developer.

How does the approval workflow work?

The system follows a strict sequence: Register → Admin Approval → Payment.

  1. A vendor submits a registration (status: Pending).
  2. An admin reviews and approves or rejects it.
  3. If approved, the vendor receives an email with a payment link.
  4. The vendor pays online via Stripe (status becomes Paid).

Vendors cannot pay until an admin approves their registration. This gives you full control over who gets a booth.

How do I approve or reject a vendor?

Open the registration from the admin dashboard and use the Approve or Reject button. The vendor is emailed automatically when you take action.

How do I know how many booth spots are left?

The admin dashboard shows availability counts for each booth type. Available = total quantity minus registrations in Approved or Paid status.

Can a vendor submit multiple registrations?

Yes. A single email address can have multiple registrations for the same or different booth types. Each is tracked independently — you must approve, reject, or cancel each one separately. Reject any accidental duplicates manually.

Is it safe for multiple admins to use the system at the same time?

Yes. The system prevents conflicts automatically. If two admins try to approve the last booth slot at the same time, only one will succeed — the other will see an error message.

Booths and Status Changes — Inventory, approvals, undoing actions, and capacity
Do Pending registrations take up booth inventory?

No. Only Approved and Paid registrations count toward inventory. Review and act on Pending registrations promptly to avoid over-committing.

What happens when I approve a vendor?

Approving a vendor immediately reserves a booth slot for them — even before they pay. If this is the last available slot, no further approvals for that booth type are possible until a slot is freed (via rejection or cancellation).

Can an approved vendor hold a spot without paying?

Yes, and this is the biggest operational risk. There is no automatic expiration on the Approved status. A vendor could hold a booth slot indefinitely without paying. Monitor approved-but-unpaid registrations and follow up or revoke approval if needed.

What if a booth type is full and I need to approve another vendor?

You cannot approve a vendor for a booth type that has no available slots. To free a slot, you must reject or cancel an existing Approved or Paid registration for that booth type.

How do I change booth types, prices, or quantities?

Go to Admin Settings. Each booth type can be edited for name, description, price, and total quantity. Price changes only affect future approvals. Already approved and paid registrations keep their original price.

Can I change a vendor's category after they register?

Yes. Open the registration detail page and use the category dropdown to correct it.

Can I undo an approval?

Yes. You can revert an Approved registration back to Pending for re-review (this frees the booth slot), or move it directly to Rejected.

Can I undo a rejection?

Yes. You can revert a Rejected registration back to Pending for re-review.

Can I undo a payment?

Not directly. The only option for a Paid registration is to Cancel it, which triggers a refund through Stripe. See the Payments section below.

Can a vendor withdraw their own registration?

Yes. Vendors can withdraw Pending or Approved registrations from their registration page. Withdrawal is permanent. If the vendor was approved, the booth slot is freed immediately. Both the vendor and all admins are notified by email. Vendors cannot withdraw after payment — only an admin can cancel a paid registration.

Payments, Fees, and Refunds — How payments, processing fees, and refunds work
Do I need to collect payments manually?

No. When you approve a vendor, they receive an email with a link to pay online via Stripe. All payments are handled automatically.

What happens when a vendor pays?

The system automatically updates the registration from Approved to Paid. The vendor receives a payment confirmation email.

A vendor says they paid but the status still shows Approved. What happened?

There may be a short delay between Stripe processing the payment and the system updating. Wait a few minutes and refresh. If it still hasn't changed, check the Stripe Dashboard to confirm the payment succeeded, and contact the developer if needed.

How do processing fees work?

The system can pass Stripe's processing fee through to vendors so the organization nets the full booth price. When enabled, the vendor sees and pays: booth fee + processing fee. To configure, go to Admin Settings under "Payment & Fees." To absorb fees yourself instead of passing them to vendors, set both the percentage and flat amount to 0.

If I change the processing fee settings, who is affected?

The fee is calculated when the vendor opens the payment page. If you change the fee settings, it affects any vendor who hasn't paid yet.

How do I issue a refund?

Open the Paid registration and use the Cancel button. You will be prompted to select a refund percentage and a reason. The refund is processed through Stripe automatically. The refund percentage applies only to the booth fee — the processing fee is not refunded.

Can I customize the refund percentage options?

Yes. Go to Admin Settings and edit the "Refund Presets" field. The default is 100,75,50,25,0. For example, if your policy only allows full or no refund, set it to 100,0. You can also set a "Refund Policy" text shown in the cancellation dialog and on the vendor's registration page.

Is there a way to cancel without issuing a refund?

Yes — select 0% when prompted for the refund percentage.

Does a "100% refund" mean the vendor gets all their money back?

The vendor receives 100% of the booth fee back. The processing fee is not refunded. Additionally, Stripe's own processing fee on the original charge is non-recoverable — that cost is absorbed by the organization.

Can I issue a refund directly in the Stripe Dashboard?

Yes, but we recommend using the app instead. If you refund directly in Stripe, the app will detect it — a full refund cancels the registration automatically, and admins are notified by email. A partial refund updates the refund amount but does not change the status.

What happens if a vendor files a chargeback?

You will receive an urgent alert email with the dispute details. You must respond to the dispute in the Stripe Dashboard before the deadline (typically 7–21 days). Missing the deadline means you automatically lose the funds.

Can I receive notifications when vendors take actions?

Yes. Go to Admin Settings and enable the notification toggles under "Email Notifications." Three options are available:

  • New registration submitted — notified when a vendor registers
  • Payment received — notified when a vendor completes payment
  • Insurance uploaded — notified when a vendor uploads an insurance document

These are off by default. Notifications are sent to all admin email addresses.

Can I prevent an email from being sent?

No. Vendor-facing emails are sent automatically and cannot be suppressed. Admin notification emails can be toggled off in Settings.

A vendor says they didn't receive an email. What should I do?

Verify the vendor's email address on their registration and ask them to check their spam folder. If the problem persists, contact the developer.

Insurance — Uploads, approval, and how it relates to registrations
How does insurance work in this system?

All vendors are required to have liability insurance. Vendors upload their insurance certificate through their registration page. Admins review and approve or revoke the document.

Is insurance tied to a specific registration?

No. Insurance is tied to the vendor's email address. If a vendor has multiple registrations, a single insurance upload covers all of them. Similarly, revoking insurance affects all of that vendor's registrations.

Is insurance approval separate from registration approval?

Yes. These are independent. A vendor can be Paid with no insurance on file — it is the admin's responsibility to follow up on insurance compliance separately.

Event Settings and Export — Dates, content, configuration, and data export
How do I update event dates, registration window, or page content?

Go to Admin Settings. You can edit the event name, dates, registration open/close dates, banner text, contact email, front-page content, vendor agreement, payment instructions, and insurance instructions.

Do settings changes take effect immediately?

Yes. Changes are reflected on vendor-facing pages immediately.

What happens when registration closes?

New vendor registrations are blocked. However, vendors can still log in to check their existing registrations, and approved vendors can still pay. Only new submissions are blocked.

Where does the banner text appear?

Banner text appears at the top of the homepage and the vendor dashboard only. Use it for time-sensitive announcements (e.g., "Payment deadline is March 15").

How do I export registration data?

Use the Export link on the dashboard. The CSV includes all registrations regardless of status, with contact info, booth details, payment info, and admin notes. Review before sharing externally, as admin notes and reversal reasons are included.
